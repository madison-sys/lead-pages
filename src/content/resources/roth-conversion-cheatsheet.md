## What's Inside

This cheatsheet gives you the exact numbers behind the bracket-fill Roth conversion strategy — the one piece of planning most pre-retirees with $500K–$2M in a traditional IRA are missing.

**What you'll find:**
- **2026 Bracket Reference (MFJ)** — the 10%, 12%, and 22%+ rates with plain-English strategy notes at each tier
- **Conversion Capacity Table** — your max annual conversion at the 12% bracket ceiling, based on what other income you already have
- **IRMAA Threshold Chart** — the exact MAGI cliff amounts for 2026 that determine your 2028 Medicare premiums, with per-person and per-couple annual cost at each tier
- **The Conversion Window** — a timeline from age 62 to 73+ showing when the window is open, narrowing, and closed
- **4-Question Eligibility Check** — a quick filter to confirm you're a candidate before running the full analysis
- **Quick Reference Table** — 10 key numbers (brackets, deductions, IRMAA cliff, RMD ages, SS thresholds) on one page

## Who This Is For

Pre-retirees and retirees ages 57–72 with a significant traditional IRA or 401(k) balance who are approaching — or currently in — the window between retirement and age 70.

This is especially useful if you've heard about Roth conversions but aren't sure how much to convert, when to stop, or how the IRMAA surcharge affects the math. It's a reference document, not a sales pitch — take it to your tax advisor and run the numbers with your actual income.

## The Number That Changes Everything

For most married filers with $500K–$2M saved, the target is the **12% bracket ceiling: $96,950 in taxable income (2026)**. Every dollar you convert below that line is taxed at 12%. Every dollar above it shifts to 22% — and crossing the $212,000 MAGI threshold adds $1,776/year in Medicare surcharges on top.

The strategy is simple: convert up to the ceiling every year you have low income — before Social Security starts, before RMDs begin, before the window closes.

The cheatsheet shows you exactly where that line is for your situation.
